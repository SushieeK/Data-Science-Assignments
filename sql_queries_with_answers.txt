
1. Total number of orders completed on 18th March 2023:
   SQL: SELECT COUNT(*) FROM SALES WHERE Date = '2023-03-18';
   Answer: 2

2. Total number of orders completed on 18th March 2023 with the first name 'John' and last name 'Doe':
   SQL: SELECT COUNT(*) FROM SALES S
        JOIN CUSTOMERS C ON S.Customer_id = C.Customer_id
        WHERE S.Date = '2023-03-18' AND C.First_name = 'John' AND C.Last_name = 'Doe';
   Answer: 0

3. Total number of customers that purchased in January 2023 and the average amount spent per customer:
   SQL: SELECT COUNT(DISTINCT Customer_id) AS Total_Customers, AVG(Total_Spend) AS Avg_Spend_Per_Customer
        FROM (SELECT Customer_id, SUM(Revenue) AS Total_Spend
              FROM SALES
              WHERE DATEPART(month, Date) = 1 AND DATEPART(year, Date) = 2023
              GROUP BY Customer_id) AS Customer_Spend;
   Answer: Total Customers: 39, Average Spend per Customer: $168.89

4. Departments that generated less than $600 in 2022:
   SQL: SELECT Department FROM ITEMS I
        JOIN SALES S ON I.Item_id = S.Item_id
        WHERE YEAR(S.Date) = 2022
        GROUP BY Department
        HAVING SUM(S.Revenue) < 600;
   Answer: None (All departments generated at least $600)
